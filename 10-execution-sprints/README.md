# 10 · Execution Sprints

Where the work actually happens. Each product gets a 14-day timeboxed sprint from "ready to start" to "live on Gumroad with a workflow." Past sprints get archived in `completed-sprints/`. The active sprint lives at `current-sprint.md`.

## Files in this folder

- [`14-day-sprint-template.md`](./14-day-sprint-template.md) — The reusable sprint format.
- [`current-sprint.md`](./current-sprint.md) — The live sprint document (currently: SuiteDash Good Parts).
- `completed-sprints/` — Archive of finished sprints. Naming: `YYYY-MM-DD-<product-slug>.md`.

## Why a 14-Day Timebox

- 14 days is short enough to force decisions and prevent perfectionism
- 14 days is long enough to ship something genuinely good
- 14 days is a unit small enough to commit to, even with a day job
- 14 days creates a natural "stop and review" cadence between products

## The Sprint Discipline

- One sprint at a time. No parallel products.
- The sprint ends on Day 14 whether the product is "perfect" or not.
- "Perfect" is the enemy. v1.0 ugly beats v2.0 unreleased.
- After Day 14, the product is live AND a 7-day review is on the calendar.

## Status Updates

Update `current-sprint.md` daily during a sprint. The daily status doesn't need to be long — 1–3 sentences on what got done, what's blocked, and what's next.
