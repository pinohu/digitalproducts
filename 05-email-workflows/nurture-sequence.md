# Nurture Sequence

The default email cadence for the email list between launches. Job: stay top-of-mind, deliver value, build trust, prime the next launch.

## Cadence

**Default: Once per week.** More frequent and you risk fatigue. Less frequent and the list goes cold.

**Format:** Newsletter-style, not transactional. ~600–1200 words per send.

## Content Mix (Weekly Rotation)

Don't write whatever feels relevant each week. Rotate intentionally so the list gets variety:

| Week | Format |
|---|---|
| Week 1 | **Tactical breakdown** — one specific framework, tool, or process from your day-to-day work |
| Week 2 | **Case study** — a customer story or a personal experiment with results |
| Week 3 | **Contrarian POV** — a take that pushes back on conventional wisdom in your space |
| Week 4 | **Behind-the-scenes** — what you're working on, what's failing, what's working |

Then loop. After 4 weeks, restart with a new tactical breakdown.

## Standard Newsletter Structure

```
Subject: [Specific outcome or curiosity hook, <50 chars]

Preview line: [Continuation of subject, ~80 chars]

Hook (1–2 sentences): The specific insight or question this email answers.

Body (3–6 paragraphs):
- Set up the problem
- Walk through the solution / framework / story
- One concrete takeaway

Close: A specific question or reply prompt.

P.S.: Either a soft reminder of a product, or a link to something useful (your blog, a tool, a tweet thread).
```

## Sales Mentions

The default ratio: **3 nurture emails for every 1 sales mention.**

Sales mentions don't have to be a full pitch — they can be a P.S., a reference inside the body ("I wrote about this in [Product Name]"), or a one-paragraph soft pitch. Hard pitches are reserved for launch sequences.

## What NOT to Send

- ❌ "Just checking in!" emails with no actual value
- ❌ Roundups of "interesting links from around the web" (no original thought = list churn)
- ❌ Emails that exist only to sell — list will tune you out
- ❌ Anything generic enough that the reader could read it on any other newsletter

## Subject Line Patterns That Work

- **Specific number:** *"The 3 SuiteDash automations I run for every client"*
- **Counterintuitive:** *"Why I stopped using [popular tool]"*
- **Question:** *"Is [common thing] actually worth it?"*
- **Story hook:** *"What a $4,200 invoice taught me about pricing"*
- **Direct outcome:** *"How to [outcome] in [timeframe]"*

## Performance Targets

| Metric | Floor | Target | Best |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 25% | 40% | 60%+ |
| Click rate | 2% | 7% | 15%+ |
| Reply rate | 0.5% | 2% | 5%+ |
| Unsubscribe rate (per send) | <0.5% | <0.3% | <0.1% |

If unsubscribe rate spikes above 0.5% on any single send, that email's content was wrong for this list. Don't repeat that pattern.

## Long-Term Goal

The nurture sequence's quiet job is to make every launch easier. Subscribers who've been on the list 6+ months convert at 3–5x the rate of fresh subscribers. The compounding asset is **earned trust over time**.
