# Abandoned Cart Workflow

Triggered when a buyer adds a product to cart, gets to checkout, and leaves without completing. Recovers 10–20% of lost sales on average.

In Gumroad, abandoned cart workflows are partially automated — Gumroad sends a default email 24 hours after abandonment with the embedded product list. We can edit the subject and body but not the timing or the embedded product. That's our one shot.

---

## The 24-Hour Abandoned Cart Email

**Subject:** Forgot something?

**First line preview:** I noticed you almost got [Product Name]...

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> I noticed you almost got [Product Name] but didn't quite finish checkout.
>
> Quick reminder of what you'd be getting:
>
> [Embedded product list — Gumroad inserts this automatically.]
>
> Founding price is still active: $[X].
>
> If something held you back — a question, a concern, anything — just hit reply. I'll answer personally.
>
> 30-day money-back guarantee, so worst case you try it and refund. No risk on your end.
>
> [Name]
>
> P.S. If you decided it's not for you, no problem. Ignore this email and you won't hear from me again about it.

---

## Why This Works

- **Acknowledges the abandonment without shaming** ("forgot something" not "you abandoned us!")
- **Lowers the barrier to ask questions** — most cart abandonment is a question they didn't know who to ask.
- **Reinforces the guarantee** — restates risk reversal at the moment of hesitation.
- **Clean exit option** — the P.S. respects "no" and prevents the email from feeling pushy.

## What NOT to Do

- ❌ Send a 7-email cart-abandonment sequence. Gumroad caps at one email; even on platforms that allow more, more than 2 emails reads as desperate.
- ❌ Offer a discount in the abandoned cart email. This trains buyers to abandon to get a discount. Founding price is the only discount.
- ❌ Use guilt language ("Don't miss out!" / "You'll regret this!"). Trust > pressure.

## Performance Targets

| Metric | Floor | Target |
|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 35% | 50%+ |
| Click rate | 10% | 20%+ |
| Recovery rate | 5% | 15%+ |

If recovery rate is below 5% consistently, the issue is upstream (sales page, pricing, offer) — not the email.
