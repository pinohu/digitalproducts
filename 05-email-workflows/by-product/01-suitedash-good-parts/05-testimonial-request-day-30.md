# Email 6 — Day 30: Testimonial Request

**Trigger:** 30 days after purchase
**Send time:** Same time of day as the welcome email

---

## Subject Line (Primary)

Did it work for you?

## Subject Line (Alt 1)

30 days in — quick favor

## Subject Line (Alt 2)

A 2-sentence favor (and a free upgrade)

---

## Preheader

Asking a small favor in exchange for a free copy of the next product.

---

## Body

> Hey [first name],
>
> It's been 30 days since you grabbed The Good Parts of SuiteDash.
>
> Quick question: did it work?
>
> If yes — would you be willing to send me a 2–3 sentence testimonial I can use on the sales page? Bonus points for a specific number or outcome. Examples of what's useful:
>
> - "Saved me 12 hours of fumbling — went from no portal to onboarding my first client in one evening."
> - "The Skip-This list alone was worth the price. Cancelled three subscriptions I was paying for SuiteDash to replace."
> - "Got my intake-to-portal automation running in 90 minutes. First client through it the next day."
>
> Just hit reply with the testimonial. In exchange, I'll send you a free copy of the next Dynasty Empire product when it ships (currently building rung 2 of the ladder — you'll get it at $0 instead of $97).
>
> If no — also reply. I want to know what didn't land. The 90-Minute Guarantee is technically expired at Day 30 but I'll honor it manually if you ask. No hard feelings, no hoops.
>
> Either way, thanks for trying it.
>
> — Ike

---

## Why This Works

- Asks at the right moment (results visible, but enthusiasm hasn't decayed)
- Three example testimonial formats = makes the ask easy and produces usable copy
- Specific incentive (free copy of next product) = motivation without feeling bought
- "If no, also reply" = builds trust even with refund-likely buyers; produces highest-signal feedback
- Honors the guarantee past its formal expiry = loyalty signal that costs little but pays back at next launch
- Typical response rate: 10–15%. At a launch run of 100 sales, that's 10–15 testimonials feeding the next launch's sales page
