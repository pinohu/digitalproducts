# Post-Purchase Email Sequence

The 6-email sequence that runs after every purchase. Drives implementation, generates testimonials, tees up the next-rung product. **This sequence runs on every product without exception.**

Implemented in Gumroad Workflows: trigger = "Customer purchases this product."

---

## Email 1 — 0 hours after purchase: Welcome + Confirm

**Subject:** Welcome — your [product name] is inside

**First line preview:** Thanks for trusting me with this. Here's the download link in case you missed it...

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> Thanks for grabbing [Product Name]. Here's the download link again, just in case: [link]
>
> Quick context on why I made this: [2–3 sentences on the personal motivation / the moment you decided this needed to exist].
>
> One ask: as you go through this, hit reply and tell me what's resonating, what's confusing, what's missing. I read every reply. Your feedback is what makes version 2 better.
>
> If you get stuck on anything, just reply to this email — I'll personally help you troubleshoot.
>
> [Name]
>
> P.S. The fastest path through this is [specific suggestion — e.g., "skip to chapter 3 first, then come back to chapter 1"]. Trust me.

**Why it works:**
- Restates the download (handles inbox-search anxiety)
- Personal context = trust signal
- Reply ask = boosts deliverability + opens feedback channel
- P.S. = the most-read line of any email, used to direct the buyer to a quick win

---

## Email 2 — Day 1: Quick Win

**Subject:** The 1 thing to do first

**First line preview:** If you only have 15 minutes today...

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> If you only have 15 minutes with [Product Name] today, here's what to do:
>
> [Specific, immediately-actionable instruction. Example: "Open page 12 — the [specific thing] checklist. Run through it on your own setup. That alone usually saves people 4–6 hours."]
>
> Don't try to read the whole thing in one sitting. Start with this. Build momentum. Come back tomorrow for the next piece.
>
> [Name]

**Why it works:**
- People often buy and never open. This pulls them in.
- Quick win = small dopamine hit, builds the habit of using your product.
- Lowered effort threshold = lower abandonment.

## Email 3 — Day 3: Common Mistakes

**Subject:** Stuck? Here's why.

**First line preview:** Most people hit one of these three walls...

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> By now you've probably hit a wall somewhere in [Product Name]. Most people hit one of these three:
>
> **Mistake #1:** [Specific common mistake] → [How to avoid it].
> **Mistake #2:** [Specific common mistake] → [How to avoid it].
> **Mistake #3:** [Specific common mistake] → [How to avoid it].
>
> If you're hitting one of these, you're normal. If you're hitting none of these and breezing through, ignore this email.
>
> Either way — reply if you hit something I didn't cover. Genuinely want to know.
>
> [Name]

**Why it works:**
- Pre-empts the "I tried it, it didn't work" refund.
- Normalizes friction = lowers self-blame.
- Reply ask again = open feedback channel.

## Email 4 — Day 7: Case Study / Use Case

**Subject:** How [name] used this to [specific outcome]

**First line preview:** Real example from someone who actually used this...

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> Wanted to share a quick story. [Beta tester / customer name] used [Product Name] last month and ended up [specific outcome with numbers].
>
> Here's what they did:
>
> [3–4 bullet points on their specific path]
>
> The key takeaway: [1-sentence lesson the reader can apply].
>
> If you're not seeing similar results yet, you might be at step [X] still. Stick with it.
>
> [Name]

**Why it works:**
- Social proof at the moment buyers might be losing momentum.
- Shows what's possible.
- Concrete = credible.

---

## Email 5 — Day 14: Soft Pitch — Next Step

**Subject:** When you're ready for what's next

**First line preview:** Most people who finish [Product Name] hit a different wall...

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> If you've been working through [Product Name], you're probably starting to bump into the next thing: [the natural next problem the buyer faces].
>
> That's the problem [Next-Rung Product] solves.
>
> No pressure — just wanted you to know it exists. If/when you're ready: [link].
>
> If you're still working through [current product], ignore this. Take your time.
>
> [Name]
>
> P.S. The 30-day guarantee is still active. If [current product] hasn't been worth it, just reply with "refund" and you're done. No hoops.

**Why it works:**
- Reminds them the next rung exists without being pushy.
- Reinforces guarantee = trust.
- "Take your time" framing = anti-salesy.

## Email 6 — Day 30: Testimonial Request

**Subject:** Did this work for you?

**First line preview:** Asking a small favor...

**Body:**

> Hey [first name],
>
> It's been 30 days since you grabbed [Product Name]. Quick question:
>
> Did it work for you?
>
> If yes — would you be willing to send me a 2-3 sentence testimonial I can use on the sales page? Bonus points if you can include a specific number or outcome. (e.g., "saved me 12 hours of setup time" or "got me from confused to deployed in 2 days").
>
> Just hit reply with the testimonial. I'll send you a free copy of [specific bonus / next product] as a thank-you.
>
> If no — also reply. I want to know what didn't land. No hard feelings, and the 30-day guarantee window is still partially open if you want a refund.
>
> Either way, thanks for trying it.
>
> [Name]

**Why it works:**
- Asks at the moment results should be visible.
- Specific testimonial format = usable copy, not generic praise.
- Free thank-you bonus = motivation.
- Acknowledges "no" answer = builds trust even if it costs a refund.
- Typical response rate: 10–15%. That's enough to fuel social proof for the next launch.

## Setup Checklist (Per Product)

In Gumroad:
- [ ] Workflow created with trigger "Customer purchases [product]"
- [ ] Email 1: 0 hours, published
- [ ] Email 2: 1 day, published
- [ ] Email 3: 3 days, published
- [ ] Email 4: 7 days, published
- [ ] Email 5: 14 days, published — link to next-rung product confirmed
- [ ] Email 6: 30 days, published — testimonial request live
- [ ] Test purchase via 100% discount code to verify all emails fire correctly
- [ ] Reply-to address goes to a monitored inbox

## Iteration

After 50 sales: review email open rates and reply rates. Rewrite the lowest-performing email. After 200 sales: A/B test subject lines on the highest-volume email (usually email 1).
