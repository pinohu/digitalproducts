# Headline Formulas

Proven headline patterns for digital product sales pages. Plug in your specifics. Don't reinvent.

---

## 1. The Outcome + Timeframe + Without

**Formula:** `[Specific outcome] in [Timeframe] without [Common pain]`

- "Deploy SuiteDash for Your Agency in 90 Minutes Without Reading 200 Pages of Docs"
- "Build a Profitable Directory in 30 Days Without Writing a Line of Code"
- "Add 10,000 LinkedIn Followers in 90 Days Without Becoming a Full-Time Content Creator"

---

## 2. The "How To" with Specificity

**Formula:** `How to [specific outcome] (even if [common limiting belief])`

- "How to Configure SuiteDash for a 6-Person Agency (Even If You've Never Used It Before)"
- "How to Sell Your First $1,000 Digital Product (Even Without an Audience)"

---

## 3. The Number + Outcome

**Formula:** `[Number] [things] that [outcome]`

- "The 7 SuiteDash Modules That Actually Drive Revenue (and the 12 You Should Skip)"
- "11 Email Sequences That Made Me $42K Last Quarter"
- "The 3 Automations That Pay Back Your SuiteDash License in Week One"

---

## 4. The Bold Promise + Proof Hook

**Formula:** `[Bold outcome]. [Proof or qualifier].`

- "Cut Your SuiteDash Setup Time From 40 Hours to 90 Minutes. Here's the Exact Path I Used to Deploy It Across 12 Client Accounts."
- "From $0 to $100K in 12 Months as a One-Person Business. The Exact Playbook."

---

## 5. The "X for Y" Specificity Hook

**Formula:** `[Generic thing] for [hyper-specific avatar]`

- "SuiteDash for Operators Who Already Know What They're Doing"
- "Email Sequences for B2B Founders Who Hate Sounding Like Marketers"
- "LinkedIn Growth for Engineers Who'd Rather Be Coding"

---

## 6. The Anti-Headline (Pattern Interrupt)

**Formula:** `[Counterintuitive statement]. [Reframe / promise].`

- "Stop Trying to Master SuiteDash. Use Only These 7 Things Instead."
- "You Don't Need More Content. You Need One Product That Sells. Here's How."

---

## 7. The Question Headline

**Formula:** `What if [you could outcome] without [pain]?`

- "What If You Could Run Your Whole Agency on a Single Platform — Without 80 Hours of Setup?"
- "What If Your First Digital Product Made $5K in Its First Week?"

---

## 8. The "Insider" / "Hidden" Hook

**Formula:** `The [hidden / insider / overlooked] [thing] that [outcome]`

- "The Overlooked SuiteDash Feature That Replaced 4 of Our Subscriptions"
- "The Hidden Pricing Trick That 3x'd Our Digital Product Revenue"

---

## 9. The Direct Comparison

**Formula:** `[X] vs. [Y]: [Outcome / verdict]`

- "SuiteDash vs. HubSpot for Solopreneurs: Why I Switched and What It Saved Me"
- "Gumroad vs. Etsy for Digital Products: A 12-Month Side-By-Side"

---

## 10. The "Tired Of" Empathy Hook

**Formula:** `Tired of [specific pain]? Here's [solution].`

- "Tired of Cobbling Together 14 Tools Just to Run a Simple Agency? Here's the One-Platform Path That Actually Works."

---

## Headline Diagnostic Checklist

Before shipping a headline, ask:

- [ ] Does it name a *specific* outcome (not "improve" or "boost")?
- [ ] Does it have a *time anchor* (90 minutes, 30 days, week one)?
- [ ] Does it address an *objection* (without X, even if Y)?
- [ ] Does it qualify the *avatar* (for [specific person])?
- [ ] Is it *under 14 words*?
- [ ] Would a *skeptical buyer* read it and not roll their eyes?

If any answer is no, rewrite.

---

## Sub-Headline Pattern

The sub-headline qualifies the buyer and adds the differentiator:

> `For [specific avatar], [specific qualifier]. [Differentiator from alternatives].`

Example:
> *For SuiteDash Pinnacle subscribers who've spent 10+ hours and still don't have a working deployment. Not another tutorial — a configured opinionated path.*

---

## Bad Headlines (Use as Reference for What Not to Do)

- ❌ "Welcome to the Ultimate Guide" → no outcome
- ❌ "Master SuiteDash" → vague, no specifics
- ❌ "Transform Your Business Today!" → hype, no substance
- ❌ "The One Thing You Need to Know About..." → curiosity-bait, no payoff in headline
- ❌ "Are You Making These Mistakes?" → fear-based, generic
- ❌ "[Industry] is Changing — Are You Ready?" → vague trend hook with no value
