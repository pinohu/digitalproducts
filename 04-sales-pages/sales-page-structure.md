# Sales Page Structure — The 12 Sections

Every digital product sales page follows this skeleton. Variations exist (long-form vs. short-form, video vs. text) but the sequence and logic are constant.

---

## Section 1 — Headline (H1)

**Job:** Capture attention in <3 seconds. State the dream outcome with specificity.

**Formula:** `[Specific outcome] + [Timeframe] + [Without the common pain]`

**Examples:**
- ❌ "Master SuiteDash" → too vague, no outcome
- ✅ "Deploy SuiteDash for Your Agency in 90 Minutes — Without Reading 200 Pages of Documentation"

**Length:** Aim for 8–14 words. If it doesn't fit on one line, it's too long.

---

## Section 2 — Sub-Headline / Deck

**Job:** Qualify the buyer ("this is for me"). State the differentiator.

**Formula:** `For [specific avatar] who want [specific outcome] without [common objection].`

**Examples:**
- ✅ "For technical operators sitting on a SuiteDash Pinnacle license, who want a configured deployment path — not another 40-hour video tutorial."

---

## Section 3 — Pain Agitation

**Job:** Show you understand their specific frustration. Mirror their internal monologue.

**Structure:**
- Where they are now (3–5 specific frustrations)
- Why what they've already tried hasn't worked
- The cost of staying stuck (time, money, missed opportunity)

**Tone:** Empathetic, not condescending. The buyer should think *"finally, someone gets it."*

**Length:** 150–300 words. Don't overdo it — agitate, then move on.

---

## Section 4 — Promise / Transformation

**Job:** Bridge from current state to desired state. Paint the *after*.

**Structure:**
- "Imagine if instead..."
- 3–5 specific markers of the new reality
- The deeper benefit (status, freedom, peace of mind)

**Avoid:** Hype, fake claims, "passive income while you sleep." Be specific and credible.

---

## Section 5 — Authority / Credibility

**Job:** Why you. Why this person delivering this product is worth listening to.

**Structure:**
- One paragraph of relevant credentials (specific, quantified)
- Why you built this product
- The story of how you figured this out (origin story = trust)

**Length:** 200–400 words. Photo helps.

---

## Section 6 — What's Inside

**Job:** Show the buyer exactly what they get. **Outcome-framed, not feature-framed.**

**Structure:** Section/module-by-module breakdown.

❌ Feature framing: *"Module 3: Email Automation"*
✅ Outcome framing: *"Module 3: How to set up the 3 automations that handle 80% of your client onboarding without you"*

**Format:** Bulleted list with bolded module titles, 1–2 sentence descriptions each.

---

## Section 7 — Bonus Stack

**Job:** Lift perceived value 3–10x. Each bonus addresses a specific objection.

**Format:**
- **Bonus #1: [Sexy Name]** — One sentence on what it is + what it does for them. *($XX value)*
- **Bonus #2: [Sexy Name]** — ... *($XX value)*
- **Bonus #3: [Sexy Name]** — ... *($XX value)*

End with: *"Total bonus value: $XXX"*

---

## Section 8 — Risk Reversal / Guarantee

**Job:** Eliminate the buyer's perceived risk.

**Format:** Pull from `/02-offers/guarantee-templates.md`. Place this in a visually distinct box. Make it loud.

> **30-Day Money-Back Guarantee.** If for any reason you don't find this useful, email me within 30 days and I'll refund every penny. No questions, no forms, no hoops.

---

## Section 9 — Social Proof / Testimonials

**Job:** Demonstrate that this works for people *like the buyer.*

**Format:** 3–8 testimonials. Each one should have:
- Real name
- Photo (real, not stock)
- Specific outcome (numbers if possible)
- Their context ("3rd-year SuiteDash user, agency of 4")

**Avoid:** Generic *"this product is amazing!"* testimonials. They look fake even when they aren't.

**For first launch:** Recruit beta testers from the audience in exchange for a free copy + testimonial. Have at least 3 ready before launch.

---

## Section 10 — Pricing

**Job:** Make the price feel like a steal compared to the value stack.

**Format — the stack-and-strike:**
> **What you get:**
> Core PDF — *($97 value)*
> Bonus #1 — *($47 value)*
> Bonus #2 — *($97 value)*
> Bonus #3 — *($47 value)*
> ──────────────
> **Total Value: $288**
>
> **Today: $49**

**Founding price banner:** *"Founding price expires [Friday at 11:59 PM EST] — then it goes to $97 for everyone after."*

---

## Section 11 — FAQ

**Job:** Defeat every remaining objection.

**Standard questions to answer:**
- "How long do I have access?"
- "What format is this delivered in?"
- "Will this work if I'm a beginner / not a beginner?"
- "How is this different from [competitor]?"
- "What if it doesn't work for me?"
- "How long does it take to go through?"
- "Can I share this with my team?"
- "Is there a refund policy?" (yes, even though it's in section 8)
- "What if I have questions after I buy?"

**Length:** 7–12 questions. Each answer 2–4 sentences.

---

## Section 12 — Final CTA + Urgency

**Job:** One last push. Clear button. Real deadline.

**Format:**
- Restate the dream outcome
- Restate the price (with the strike-through)
- Restate the guarantee
- One clear button: *"Get [Product Name] for $49 →"*
- Urgency line: *"Founding price expires [date]"*

**No additional links below this.** The CTA is the last thing on the page. Period.

---

## Layout Principles

- **One column.** Multi-column sales pages convert worse. Keep the eye flowing down.
- **Mobile-first.** ~70% of buyers will read this on a phone. Test there first.
- **Strategic bolding.** 3–5 bolded phrases per screen. Bold the things a skimmer would need.
- **One CTA button per ~500 words.** Buyers should never have to scroll up to find the buy button.
- **No autoplaying video.** Video below the fold optional. Page should work with video off.

## What Not to Include

- ❌ Generic "About Us" links pulling them away from the page
- ❌ Multiple competing CTAs (newsletter signup + buy button = neither happens)
- ❌ Pop-ups asking for email when the user is trying to read the sales page
- ❌ Live chat widgets that interrupt the read

The sales page has one job: convert. Anything that competes with that job is a leak.
