# Bonus 3 — The Kill List: 12 SuiteDash Features to Ignore Until Later

The fastest way to make SuiteDash useful is to stop treating every module as equally urgent.

Use this list before you add another feature to your first deployment. A feature is not “bad” just because it is on the kill list. It is only bad when it enters before the workflow needs it.

---

## The v1 rule

Do not configure a feature unless it supports one of these jobs:

1. Capturing the client relationship.
2. Collecting the right intake data.
3. Giving the client one clear portal path.
4. Running the delivery/project workflow.
5. Handling files, documents, proposals, invoices, or payments.
6. Automating one repeated handoff.

Everything else waits for a trigger.

---

## 1. LMS / course area

### Why it tempts you

It feels like a way to turn your client portal into an education hub.

### Why to skip it now

Most first deployments need onboarding clarity, not a full training library.

### Add it when

Clients repeatedly need the same training assets and you can name the curriculum.

---

## 2. Full email newsletter/autoresponder stack

### Why it tempts you

SuiteDash can centralize communication, so it feels wasteful to keep another email tool.

### Why to skip it now

Your first deployment needs transactional and workflow emails, not a full audience-nurture machine.

### Add it when

You have a defined buyer/customer segment and a repeatable sequence that belongs inside SuiteDash.

---

## 3. Advanced white-label design

### Why it tempts you

Branding makes the portal feel “done.”

### Why to skip it now

A pretty portal with unclear next steps still fails.

### Add it when

A real client has used the workflow and you know which pages matter.

---

## 4. Complex dashboard customization

### Why it tempts you

Dashboards look like control.

### Why to skip it now

Until the workflow runs, dashboard widgets are guesses.

### Add it when

You know the 3-5 metrics that drive owner/client decisions.

---

## 5. Deep financial reporting

### Why it tempts you

Revenue reporting feels strategic.

### Why to skip it now

Your first job is to get the invoice/payment handoff working. Reporting can wait.

### Add it when

SuiteDash has enough real payment/invoice data to report on.

---

## 6. Staff time tracking / time clock

### Why it tempts you

It promises accountability.

### Why to skip it now

For many solo/small-team deployments, time tracking adds compliance drag before it adds value.

### Add it when

Billing, payroll, capacity planning, or client reporting actually depends on tracked time.

---

## 7. Full help desk / ticketing

### Why it tempts you

Support feels like a natural client-portal feature.

### Why to skip it now

A structured request form plus assigned task may solve the v1 problem.

### Add it when

Support volume is high enough that requests need categories, SLAs, escalation, and reporting.

---

## 8. Multi-department permissions

### Why it tempts you

Role-based access feels professional and safe.

### Why to skip it now

Complex permissions slow setup and create QA risk when the team is small.

### Add it when

Multiple roles need materially different access to client records, financials, files, or project work.

---

## 9. Fancy portal pages

### Why it tempts you

You want the portal to impress clients.

### Why to skip it now

Clients need the next step, files, status, and invoice/payment more than a custom brochure page.

### Add it when

You have real client behavior showing which portal pages they use.

---

## 10. Broad historical CRM migration

### Why it tempts you

A complete database feels like a clean start.

### Why to skip it now

Migrating messy history before deciding field rules creates cleanup work.

### Add it when

Your v1 record schema is stable and you know which historical records matter.

---

## 11. Complex external integrations

### Why it tempts you

SuiteDash plus n8n/Make/Zapier can connect almost everything.

### Why to skip it now

External integrations amplify messy process design. If the manual handoff is unclear, automation spreads the mess.

### Add it when

The native workflow is proven and the external handoff saves repeated work.

---

## 12. Multiple service workflows at once

### Why it tempts you

You want SuiteDash to become the full operating system immediately.

### Why to skip it now

Every additional workflow multiplies fields, portals, templates, automations, QA, and edge cases.

### Add it when

The first workflow has run with a real client and the next workflow shares enough structure to reuse the setup.

---

## Final keep/kill question

Before configuring any feature, ask:

> Does this make the first client workflow faster, clearer, safer, or more visible?

If no, it belongs on the kill list for now.
