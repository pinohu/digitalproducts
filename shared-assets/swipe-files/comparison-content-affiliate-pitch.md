# Comparison-Content Affiliate Pitch — Outreach Template

> *Reusable across products. First deployed for "The Good Parts of SuiteDash" (Ahsuite, Capterra alternatives, "SuiteDash vs ___" blog owners). Same template works for any product where evaluation-stage buyers land on third-party comparison content.*

---

## When to Use This Template

- Pre-launch or post-launch outreach to bloggers, newsletter operators, and review-site owners whose content ranks for buyer-intent comparison searches.
- Specifically, content like *"X vs Y"*, *"alternatives to X"*, *"is X worth it"*, *"X review"*, where a buyer in evaluation mode would land.
- Bypasses the standard "after 50 sales" affiliate program rule (`/09-iteration-and-scale/affiliate-program.md`) for this narrow case — comparison-content owners promote without needing prior social proof, because the alignment between their audience and your offer is mechanical, not reputational.

---

## The Email

**Subject lines (test 2):**

A. *Affiliate offer — 40% on a $49 SuiteDash PDF*
B. *Quick question about your [specific post title]*

A is direct and works for owners with high inbox volume. B has higher open rates but slightly higher mismatch risk. Default to A; switch to B if open rates underperform.

**Body:**

> Hi [Name],
>
> Read your [specific post — *"What is SuiteDash"* or *"SuiteDash vs Ahsuite"* etc.] post — solid breakdown, especially the part about [one specific thing they covered well]. You're ranking for the exact buyer-intent keywords I care about.
>
> I just shipped a $49 PDF called *The 23 SuiteDash Features You Actually Need (And The 177 To Ignore For Now)* — opinionated guide for people who just signed up and feel buried by the platform. It's pulled from running 136 Pinnacle licenses across my own portfolio.
>
> Want to set up a 40% affiliate split? Your readers are already sorted to the right intent, the price point is low enough to be a no-brainer add-on, and a 60-day cookie covers the slow research → buy cycle SuiteDash buyers tend to follow.
>
> I'll send you a free copy first — no commitment. If it's not a fit, no hard feelings. Reply with the email you want it sent to and I'll fire it over.
>
> Best,
> Ike Ohu
> [Gumroad link]

## Personalization Required

Don't send this without filling all four bracketed slots:

1. **[Name]** — first name only. Verify spelling.
2. **[specific post title]** — the actual title of the post that's ranking. Linking in your reply is fine.
3. **[one specific thing they covered well]** — pull a real detail from the post. *"the comparison table where you broke out scheduling features"* or *"the screenshot walkthrough of the Circles permission setup."* Generic compliments fail.
4. **[Gumroad link]** — your direct product link, not a redirect.

If you can't fill slot 3 with something real, don't send to that recipient. They'll smell the boilerplate.

---

## Volume Discipline

- **5 emails per week, max.** Comparison-content site owners talk to each other (smaller scene than you'd think). Mass blast = bad reputation.
- **Track replies.** A 30%+ reply rate is normal for genuine outreach. Below 10% means the personalization isn't landing.
- **Follow up once at +7 days.** Single reply: *"Just bumping this up — happy to discuss any way at all, even if just trading links."* No second follow-up.

---

## Affiliate Terms (Default)

- **40% commission** for the first 30 days from launch (or first 30 days from the affiliate's first link click — match Gumroad's tracking model).
- **30% standing commission** thereafter.
- **60-day cookie** (configure in Gumroad).
- **Monthly payout**, after the standard 30-day refund window.
- **No exclusivity required** — affiliates can promote competing products. Saying so up front builds trust.

These terms are documented at `/09-iteration-and-scale/affiliate-program.md` for the default Dynasty-wide program. The 40%-for-30-days bump is the launch-window incentive specific to comparison-content affiliates.

## What's In the Free Copy You Send

When the recipient replies "send it":

1. The PDF + 3 bonuses (full product, no watermark)
2. A **swipe file** with 3 pre-written affiliate-promotion options:
   - One-paragraph blog footer note
   - One-tweet/thread version
   - One-line email mention
3. Your direct Gumroad affiliate signup link
4. An offer to schedule a 15-minute call if they want to discuss positioning

Don't make them write copy from scratch. Affiliates who have to write copy promote 5x less than affiliates who get it pre-written.

## What Not to Promise

- Don't promise to mention them publicly in exchange for affiliate participation. That's two-way obligation; keeps the relationship transactional.
- Don't promise custom landing pages or co-branded versions. The point is leverage; custom work kills it.
- Don't promise top placement on your site in exchange for affiliate signup. Reciprocal links are fine; ranked endorsements are reputation risk.
