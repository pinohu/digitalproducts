## The Gap Analysis

After reviewing all competitors, where is the empty space?

- **Format gap:** Is everyone selling video courses? We sell PDFs. Vice versa.
- **Audience gap:** Is everyone targeting beginners? We target operators. Or vice versa.
- **Depth gap:** Is everyone surface-level? We go deep on one specific thing.
- **Specificity gap:** Is everyone generic? We niche down to a specific tool / role / industry.
- **Price gap:** Is everyone at $9 or $499? We hit $49 trust tripwire and $497 system pack.

## Positioning Statement

After the gap analysis, fill in:

> *Unlike [competitor pattern], our product [unique angle], for [specific buyer], who wants [specific outcome] without [common pain].*

Example:
> *Unlike generic SuiteDash tutorials that cover every feature equally, "The Good Parts of SuiteDash" surfaces only the 20% of features that drive 80% of the value, for technical operators who want to deploy fast without sitting through 40 hours of video.*

## Reverse Engineering (When Relevant)

If a competitor is doing $X/month with their version:
- What are they doing well that we should adopt?
- What are they doing poorly that becomes our positioning advantage?
- What would force their existing customers to switch to ours?

## Anti-Pattern Check

- [ ] We are *not* trying to be a cheaper version of an existing product (race to the bottom).
- [ ] We are *not* trying to be a slightly-better version (no compelling reason to switch).
- [ ] We *are* claiming a clearly different angle, format, audience, or depth.
