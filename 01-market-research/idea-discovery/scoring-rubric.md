# The 50-Point Scoring Rubric

The standardized scoring system for digital product ideas. Five dimensions, ten points each, total 50.

## The Five Dimensions

| Dimension | Points | What It Measures |
|---|---|---|
| 1. **Massive Pain** | 0–10 | How badly the buyer wants the problem fixed |
| 2. **Purchasing Power** | 0–10 | How readily the buyer can pay $49–$497 |
| 3. **Easy to Target** | 0–10 | How identifiable and reachable the audience is |
| 4. **Growing Market** | 0–10 | Whether the underlying demand is rising |
| 5. **Personal Fit / Unfair Advantage** | 0–10 | Whether *you* are uniquely positioned to ship this |

**Total possible: 50.**

## Decision Thresholds

| Score | Decision |
|---|---|
| 40–50 | **Ship it.** Move directly to validation worksheet → sprint slot |
| 30–39 | **Strong candidate.** Validate with pre-sale or waitlist before sprint |
| 25–29 | **Backlog.** Hold for re-scoring quarterly; build only if higher-scoring options dry up |
| 15–24 | **Weak.** Park in backlog only if a path to fixing the weak dimensions is visible |
| 0–14 | **Kill.** Don't build. Don't backlog. Note the reason and move on |

If your scoring rubric produces 5+ ideas at 40+ in a single mining pass, the rubric is too generous — recalibrate downward. The median idea should sit around 20–25.

## Dimension 1: Massive Pain (0–10)

**Question:** How badly does the buyer want this problem solved?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 10 | Buyer says they'd pay $1K+ to fix it today. They've spent real money on failed attempts. The problem is named explicitly in their own words across multiple sources. |
| 8 | Buyer says they'd pay several hundred dollars. They've tried 2+ solutions. Pattern recurs across 10+ threads/posts. |
| 6 | Buyer is annoyed. They've complained but not yet spent money. Pattern visible in 3-5 sources. |
| 4 | Mild frustration. The problem exists but the buyer hasn't escalated. |
| 2 | Theoretical pain. Reasonable to say it's a problem, but no behavioral evidence. |
| 0 | No actual pain. You're inventing a need. |

**Test:** Find at least 3 verbatim quotes from real people describing this problem in their own words. If you can't find 3, the score is ≤ 4.

---

## Dimension 2: Purchasing Power (0–10)

**Question:** Can this audience pay $49–$497 without thinking hard about it?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 10 | B2B context, expensable, audience already buying $100+ tools/services regularly. SaaS founders, agency owners, enterprise consultants. |
| 8 | B2B-adjacent or professional context. Solopreneurs at $50K+ revenue, freelancers with steady clients, technical professionals. |
| 6 | Side-hustle / serious-hobbyist context. They have disposable income but every $99 is a deliberation. |
| 4 | Hobbyist or aspirational. They want to spend but rarely do. |
| 2 | Pre-revenue, students, or strict budget contexts. |
| 0 | Audience cannot or will not pay for a digital product at this price. |

**Test:** Identify 3 paid products this audience is already buying at $49+. If you can't, the score is ≤ 4.

---

## Dimension 3: Easy to Target (0–10)

**Question:** Can you identify and reach this audience?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 10 | Audience congregates in 5+ specific named places (subreddits, LinkedIn niches, communities, podcasts, newsletters). You can name them immediately. |
| 8 | Audience has 3-4 clear watering holes. You know how to find them. |
| 6 | Audience exists but is scattered. You'd need to use 2-3 channels to reach them. |
| 4 | You'd need paid ads or significant outbound to reach them. |
| 2 | Audience is theoretical — you can describe them but not point to where they gather. |
| 0 | "Everyone on the internet" / "small business owners" / similar non-targeting. |

**Test:** Name 5 specific places this audience already gathers. If you can't, the score is ≤ 4.

---

## Dimension 4: Growing Market (0–10)

**Question:** Is the underlying demand expanding or shrinking?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 10 | Search volume / community size / industry revenue all up 30%+ in the last 24 months. Trend is accelerating. |
| 8 | Clear upward trajectory. Growth is steady, well-documented. |
| 6 | Stable. Not growing fast, but not declining. |
| 4 | Mixed signals. Some indicators up, others flat. |
| 2 | Flat or slightly declining. |
| 0 | Demonstrably shrinking. Avoid. |

**Test:** Pull Google Trends data + community member count growth + 1 quantitative third-party signal. If you can't find at least 2 growth signals, the score is ≤ 4.

---

## Dimension 5: Personal Fit / Unfair Advantage (0–10)

**Question:** Are *you* uniquely positioned to ship this?

| Score | Description |
|---|---|
| 10 | You've personally lived the problem, deeply. You have proprietary knowledge, infrastructure, audience access, or relationships that 99% of competitors don't have. You can credibly speak to the audience. |
| 8 | You've done relevant work in the space. Your authority is provable. The product is a natural extension of work you're already doing. |
| 6 | You can produce the product credibly with research. No unfair advantage, but no major weakness either. |
| 4 | You'd be learning the topic alongside the buyer. The product would be honest but not authoritative. |
| 2 | This is far outside your experience. You'd need significant outside help. |
| 0 | You're the wrong person to ship this. Refer it to someone else or kill the idea. |

**Test:** Can you write 3 paragraphs of useful content on this topic right now, without research? If yes, score 6+. If no, score ≤ 4.

## The Scoring Process

For each candidate idea:

1. **Open this rubric.** Don't score from memory.
2. **Score each dimension separately.** Don't average / shortcut.
3. **Write a one-sentence justification** for each score. ("Score: 7. Justification: 4 verbatim quotes from r/SaaS within the last 30 days.")
4. **Total the score.**
5. **Check the decision threshold.**
6. **Append to `idea-backlog.md`** with all five dimension scores + total + decision.

Total time per idea: 3–5 minutes. Don't spend longer — if it takes 15 minutes, you're rationalizing a weak idea into a higher score.

## Scoring Inflation: How to Spot It

Run this check quarterly. If any of these are true, the rubric is being applied too generously:

- ❌ Median idea in the backlog scores >25 (median should sit at 20–25)
- ❌ More than 30% of ideas score 40+ (top tier should be 10–15% of backlog)
- ❌ You haven't killed an idea (scored ≤14) in the last 30 days
- ❌ Every idea is a "personal fit 8+" (you can't be uniquely positioned for everything)

When inflation appears: re-score the top 10 backlog ideas, deliberately looking for reasons to lower scores. The honest backlog usually loses 30–50% of items in the recalibration.

## When the Rubric Disagrees With Your Gut

This will happen. The rubric will say *"score 22, kill it"* and your gut will say *"but I really think this would work."*

Two cases:

**Case A: Your gut is right.** This is rare but real. Override the rubric only if you can identify a specific, articulate reason — usually that the rubric is missing context (e.g., "this scored 6 on personal fit, but I'm one of three people in the world with this specific knowledge"). Document the override reason. Do not override more than once a quarter.

**Case B: Your gut is wrong.** This is more common. The rubric exists precisely to override your gut when your gut is wrong. Trust the rubric. Move on.

If you find yourself overriding 2+ times per quarter, the rubric is wrong — recalibrate it. Don't override silently.
