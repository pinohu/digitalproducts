# Distribution Findings — The Good Parts of SuiteDash

> *Created: 2026-05-08. Findings from a buyer-density and watering-hole audit conducted while finalizing the launch plan. Triggered the channel-mix rebalance documented in the watering-holes addendum.*

---

## TL;DR

**Original assumption:** LinkedIn-primary, per the system-wide platform strategy.

**Finding:** For *this product's audience* (SuiteDash license holders + AppSumo LTD overflow), LinkedIn density is too thin to be the primary channel. The buyer concentration is on YouTube tutorial comment threads, G2/Capterra Q&A sections, AppSumo LTD Facebook groups, Upwork buy-side searches, and SuiteDash-comparison content (e.g., Ahsuite blog posts).

**Decision:** Demote LinkedIn from primary to secondary/opportunistic for this product specifically. Keep the system-wide LinkedIn-primary default for products whose audience density is actually on LinkedIn (e.g., MSEAM dean-level memos, UX research portfolio launches).

This is a **per-product platform decision**, not a system-wide pivot.

## The Six Watering Holes (Ranked)

Ranked by buyer density × intent. Full operational detail in `/06-launch-playbooks/by-product/01-suitedash-good-parts-watering-holes.md`.

### 1. SuiteDash YouTube tutorial comment threads (highest ROI)

Mike Lambert's "Starting from ZERO" and "Power of SuiteDash" series have active commenters in active learning mode — exactly our avatar mid-struggle. **Value-first commenting only**, never link-dropping. Profile bio funnels to Gumroad. Doubles as market research for PDFs 2–7 (the confused-commenter questions reveal which features confuse people most).

### 2. G2 + Capterra Q&A sections

1,200+ combined reviews. Both platforms surface buyer-evaluation Q&A ("is the learning curve hard?", "how long does setup take?"). Substantive answers under your name with a profile that links to your site. Buyers read these *before* buying SuiteDash; the PDF is a natural day-one purchase.

### 3. AppSumo LTD Facebook groups

The "I bought SuiteDash on AppSumo and never deployed it" segment is the literal target avatar from the sales page. Three largest groups: AppSumo NSFW Deals, Lifetime Deals on AppSumo and Beyond, Saaszilla. Drop the free first chapter excerpt with permission per group rules.

### 4. Upwork productized service

Buyers searching "SuiteDash help" on Upwork are budget-stratified. Sub-$400-budget buyers have nowhere to go (Senseis priced too high). One Upwork Project Catalog listing — "PDF + 30-min Q&A — $97" — captures that tier and feeds Upwork-public testimonials. See `/03-products/01-suitedash-good-parts/upwork-productized-listing.md`.

### 5. Comparison-content backlinks (e.g., Ahsuite)

Posts comparing SuiteDash to alternatives get organic traffic from buyers in evaluation mode. Email 5 comparison-post owners (Ahsuite, Capterra alternatives pages, "SuiteDash vs HoneyBook" type posts) with a 40% affiliate offer. Their traffic is already sorted to buyer-intent. Template at `/shared-assets/swipe-files/comparison-content-affiliate-pitch.md`.

### 6. Reddit (r/Entrepreneur, r/agency, r/smallbusiness, r/ClientPortal)

Lower volume than the others but threads keep ranking on Google for years. Search "SuiteDash" across those subs. Find threads where someone asked and got mid answers. Reply substantively, profile bio funnels.

## Why LinkedIn Stays in the Mix (Just Demoted)

LinkedIn isn't deleted from the playbook. It stays as a **secondary channel for credibility documentation** — the launch announcement, the Day-8 transparency post, the case-study-with-numbers post — because Ike's broader Dynasty audience IS on LinkedIn even though the SuiteDash buyer audience isn't. LinkedIn is where Ike documents wins for people interested in *how Dynasty operates*, not where SuiteDash buyers come from.

This distinction matters for PDFs 2–7. Some will have audiences that DO concentrate on LinkedIn (anything UX-research-adjacent, anything operator/founder-tier strategy). Others won't. Run this same density audit per product.

## Replicability Note for PDFs 2–7

Run this same audit before locking the launch playbook for each subsequent PDF. Three questions:

1. Where is the buyer DENSITY for this audience? (count the actual numbers — followers, group sizes, review counts)
2. Where is the buyer INTENT? (where do they go when they're ready to buy?)
3. Does the system-default LinkedIn-primary assumption hold for this product specifically?

If the answers don't justify LinkedIn as primary, write a distribution-findings.md and rebalance. The launch playbook template at `/06-launch-playbooks/14-day-launch-template.md` should support either channel mix.
