# AppSumo Business Factory Map

Snapshot date: 2026-05-08

This document turns the active AppSumo portfolio into a business-building routing system for Hermes, Paperclip, and any managed agents.

The point is not to treat the portfolio as 300 separate tools. The point is to organize it into repeatable business machines that can be used to launch, operate, and scale products.

This document focuses on the non-design stack. The design stack lives in `00-foundation/operator-system/design-tool-routing.md`.

## Tiering

### Tier 1: Core money-making infrastructure

These categories should get the most operator attention because they can create revenue the fastest:

1. Automation, integration, and RPA
2. CRM, client portals, and business operations
3. Lead generation, prospecting, and outbound
4. Email, SMS, and customer messaging
5. Website, app, no-code, backend, and development tools
6. SEO, writing, publishing, and authority content

### Tier 2: Growth and scale tools

These categories help accelerate businesses after the core offer and delivery system exist:

1. Social media and ad operations
2. Video and audio content
3. Analytics and conversion optimization
4. Reputation and feedback systems
5. Affiliate and referral systems
6. Reporting and dashboard surfaces

### Tier 3: Operational support tools

These matter, but should plug into workflows rather than dominate roadmap time:

1. Document automation and file workflows
2. Finance and accounting
3. Meetings and webinars
4. Security and privacy
5. Training and template libraries
6. Productivity utilities

## Workbook 90-day execution sequence

The workbook also provides a clean activation order for turning the portfolio into a practical business factory:

1. Weekend / 1-2 days
   - Create the control map and choose one pilot.
   - Expected output: one prioritized pilot such as `Erie.pro`, `LeadOS`, or `YourDeputy`.
2. Week 1
   - Launch the minimum viable lead engine.
   - Focus operating systems: `Local Lead Generation OS` and `Sales Outreach & Campaign OS`.
   - Deploy first: `Google Maps Scraper`, `Reoon Email Verifier`, `SalesNexus`, `Acumbamail`, `SuiteDash`.
3. Week 2
   - Create the conversion surface.
   - Focus operating systems: `Directory Launch OS` and `Micro-SaaS Launch OS`.
   - Deploy first: `Brilliant Directories` or `CubeWP`, `Claritee`, `Microthemer`, `Formaloo` or `GoZen`.
4. Week 3
   - Wire operations and automation.
   - Focus operating systems: `Automation Backbone OS` and `Client Portal & Service Delivery OS`.
   - Deploy first: `Activepieces`, `Boost.space`, `SuiteDash`, `Crove`, `Trafft`.
5. Week 4
   - Add content and credibility.
   - Focus operating systems: `Authority Site & Affiliate OS` and `Content Repurposing & Media OS`.
   - Deploy first: `NeuronWriter`, `Katteb`, `SUPERMACHINE`, `FlexClip`, `Story Chief`.
6. Month 2
   - Optimize and scale.
   - Focus operating system: `Analytics, Optimization & Governance OS`.
   - Deploy first: `Plerdy`, `TruConversion`, `Labrika`, `Screpy`, `OWOX BI`, `BackupSheep`.

## Decision Rules

- Do not organize tools alphabetically when making operating decisions; organize them by job-to-be-done.
- Prefer a small number of default tools per engine rather than spreading work across every available alternative.
- Route design decisions through `00-foundation/operator-system/design-tool-routing.md`.
- Route non-design tool selection through this document and the access-readiness docs in `01-market-research/appsumo/`.
- Treat `SuiteDash`, `Activepieces`, `Boost.space`, `KonnectzIT`, `Albato`, `Formaloo`, `AITable.ai`, `Emailit`, and `Brilliant Directories` as high-attention operator surfaces.
- When choosing what to operationalize first, start with the five highest-concentration operating systems from the workbook before spending much time on lower-density support categories.
- When a tool is not yet verified for real access, fall back to the access plan and ledger before assuming it is production-ready.
