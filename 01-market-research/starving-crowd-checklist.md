# Starving Crowd Checklist

Every product must pass all four criteria before we build. No exceptions. This is the gate.

## The Four Criteria

### 1. Massive Pain
The problem keeps the buyer up at night. They've tried other solutions. They're frustrated.

**Test:** Would they pay $500 to make this problem disappear today, even if our product is $49?
**Pass:** Yes.
**Fail:** "Maybe, depends..."

---

### 2. Purchasing Power
The audience can actually pay $49+ without thinking about it. B2B beats B2C beats hobbyist.

**Test:** Does the buyer use this for work, or for a side project / hobby?
**Pass:** Work — the cost is rounded to "expense it."
**Fail:** Hobby — every $19 is a deliberation.

---

### 3. Easy to Target
We can identify where they congregate. We can buy their attention or earn it organically.

**Test:** Can we name 5+ specific watering holes (subreddits, LinkedIn niches, communities, podcasts, newsletters) where this audience already gathers?
**Pass:** Yes, list them.
**Fail:** "Everywhere on the internet."

---

### 4. Growing
The market is expanding, not shrinking. There's tailwind.

**Test:** Has Google Trends / search volume / community membership grown over the past 24 months?
**Pass:** Yes, with data.
**Fail:** Flat or declining, or you can't tell.

## The Three Evergreen Markets

If your idea doesn't ladder up to one of these, reconsider:

1. **Health** — physical, mental, longevity, fitness, nutrition.
2. **Wealth** — making money, saving money, business, investing.
3. **Relationships** — personal, professional, family, dating.

Dynasty Empire's primary market is **Wealth**, sub-niche: *technical operators monetizing infrastructure they already own.*

## Anti-Patterns

These are markets that *look* attractive but consistently fail:

- "Help small business owners with [generic thing]" — too broad, low willingness to pay
- "Productivity for everyone" — saturated, low pain
- "Mindset / motivation" — low willingness to pay for info, high refund rates
- "Crypto / Web3" — high pain, but unstable purchasing power and adversarial buyers
- "Side hustle for beginners" — anti-avatar, attracts low-quality buyers
