# Catalog Roadmap

The multi-product portfolio plan. How a single PDF becomes 7 PDFs becomes a system becomes a recurring-revenue ecosystem.

## The Ladder, Restated

```
Year 1, Q1–Q2:    1–2 products live
Year 1, Q3–Q4:    3–5 products live + first bundle
Year 2, Q1–Q2:    6–8 products live + 2nd bundle + first variations
Year 2, Q3–Q4:    Premium pack ($297+) + DWY service ($997)
Year 3:           Cohort / community ($999/yr+) + DFY service ($5K+)
```

This is the realistic pace for a solo creator with a day job. Faster is possible. Slower is normal.

## Catalog Composition Targets

| Phase | Products | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| Months 1–3 | 1 | Validate the avatar, the price point, the funnel |
| Months 4–6 | 2–3 | Test breadth of the avatar's pain points |
| Months 7–9 | 3–4 | Bundle the proven products |
| Months 10–12 | 4–6 | Variations + premium tier |
| Year 2 | 6–10 | Higher-rung offerings |

## Cross-Selling Logic

Each product on its sales page references *one* other product as the natural next step. Avoid linking to all products from every page — that's overwhelming.

Pattern:
- Product 1's sales page references Product 2
- Product 2's sales page references Product 3
- Etc.
- The bundle is referenced from all products

## Anti-Cannibalization

When launching a new product, make sure it doesn't cannibalize an existing product. If Product 5 covers material that's already in Product 2, buyers will feel cheated.

The fix:
- Make Product 5 deeper / narrower than Product 2
- Position Product 5 as "the next step after Product 2"
- Bundle them together rather than offering them as competing options

## When to Retire a Product

If a product:
- Sells <5 copies/month after 6 months
- Has a refund rate >10%
- No longer reflects the brand's positioning

Retire it gracefully:
- Remove from the storefront (don't delete — buyers still need access)
- Notify existing buyers if there's a relevant successor
- Keep the URL active, redirect to the successor product

## Strategic Diversification

After 5 products in the same niche, consider:
- A second sub-niche under the same brand
- A premium tier (paid newsletter, cohort, community)
- A service offering (DWY/DFY) for the highest-LTV buyers

Don't diversify too early. The first 5 products should all serve the same avatar with adjacent products.

## The 12-Product Limit

Beyond 12 products, the catalog gets noisy. Buyers have too many choices, support load grows, and individual products get less attention.

When approaching 12: consolidate. Bundle aggressively. Sunset weaker performers. Prefer depth over breadth from there forward.
