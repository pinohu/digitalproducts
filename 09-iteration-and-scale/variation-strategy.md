# Variation Strategy

A single product, repackaged for adjacent niches. The lowest-effort, highest-leverage way to extend the value of a successful product.

## The Concept

You have one core product: "The Good Parts of SuiteDash."

You can spin variations:
- "The Good Parts of SuiteDash for Coaches"
- "The Good Parts of SuiteDash for Agencies"
- "The Good Parts of SuiteDash for Service Pros"
- "The Good Parts of SuiteDash for Real Estate Teams"

Each variation reuses 70–80% of the core content but tailors:
- The introduction (why this matters for *this* niche)
- The case studies (specific to *this* niche)
- The configuration recommendations (this niche's specific use cases)
- The cover art and headline (this niche's language)

## When to Run a Variation

Once the original has 100+ sales AND you can identify a specific sub-niche where:
- The avatar is meaningfully different
- The use case is different enough to warrant its own framing
- The audience can't easily be reached via the original positioning

## Variation Production Time

The first variation: ~10–15 hours (rewriting intro, finding new case studies, repositioning).

Each subsequent variation: ~3–5 hours (the framework is in place, you're just swapping context).

The math: a variation that takes 5 hours and generates 50 sales at $49 = $2,450 in revenue for one workday. Per-hour value: ~$490/hour. Few activities beat this.

## Variation Pricing

Same as the original. $49.

Bundling considerations:
- A buyer of "Coaches" version might want the "Agencies" version too
- Bundle pricing for variations: 2 variations at $79 (vs $98 unbundled), 3 at $109

## Variation Sales Pages

Each variation gets its own sales page with:
- Niche-specific headline
- Niche-specific testimonials
- Niche-specific bonuses (often a niche-specific checklist or template)
- Same core offer structure

## When NOT to Variate

- The original product is failing (variations don't fix a weak product)
- The "variation" would be cosmetic (same buyer, same use case, just a different label)
- The team can't sustainably manage multiple sales pages, multiple email workflows

## The Honesty Bar

Variations work as long as they're *genuinely* tailored for the niche. If the variation is just the original with the cover changed, buyers will notice and resent it.

The right amount of variation: 20–30% of content is niche-specific. The rest is the universal core.
