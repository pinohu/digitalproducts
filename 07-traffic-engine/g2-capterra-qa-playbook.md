# G2 + Capterra Q&A Playbook

> *Companion playbook to YouTube Comments and LinkedIn. G2 and Capterra are review-aggregation platforms with built-in Q&A sections that surface in Google search results for buyer-intent queries. Substantive answers under your verified profile build authority that compounds for years.*

---

## Why This Channel Matters

Buyers researching a software purchase typically land on G2 and Capterra before they commit. They read reviews, but they ALSO read the Q&A: questions like *"Is the learning curve steep?"*, *"How long does setup take?"*, *"Can I integrate with X?"* The answers to these questions are written by other users — and frequently by *vendors trying to defend their reputation.*

Your opportunity: write the BEST answer to existing buyer questions. Your profile shows your name and a link to your site. Buyers reading the Q&A who need more help will click. The traffic is small but high-intent.

This isn't a primary distribution channel. It's a credibility and SEO play. The benefit compounds because Q&A answers stay live indefinitely and rank in Google search results.

---

## When This Channel Wins

| Use this channel when... | Skip when... |
|---|---|
| Your product helps buyers of a specific software product (CRM, accounting, project mgmt, etc.) | Your product is for general operators, not tool-buyers |
| The relevant tool has 500+ reviews on G2 OR Capterra | The tool is too small (<100 reviews) — Q&A activity won't be there |
| The tool's Q&A section has unanswered or weakly-answered questions | The Q&A is fully covered by the vendor with marketing-tone answers |
| You have direct hands-on experience with the tool (you can answer credibly) | You only know the tool secondhand |

For "The Good Parts of SuiteDash": the SuiteDash G2 page has 595 reviews and Capterra has 607 — well above threshold. SuiteDash itself is HIPAA-compliant which means there's a population of healthcare-side reviewers asking questions vendors don't always answer well. Sweet spot.

## Account Setup

You need a verified reviewer account on each platform before you can answer Q&A. Start this 7+ days before launch — verification can take days.

**G2:**
- Sign up with a business email (Gmail/personal email gets flagged)
- Submit a review of SuiteDash to get verified status (~24-48 hours)
- Profile fields to fill: full name, company (PNR Holdings or Dynasty Empire), role, bio with one link
- Photo: clean headshot
- Single link: ikeohu.com (preferred) or your Gumroad pre-sell page

**Capterra:**
- Same flow as G2 (Capterra is owned by Gartner; the verification standard is similar)
- Submit a review to verify
- Profile setup with the same field treatment

**Important:** Your reviews of SuiteDash should be honest, balanced, and specific. A 4-star review with concrete pros and cons converts vastly better than a 5-star puff piece. Buyers can smell vendor-shilling.

## Finding the Right Q&A to Answer

Both G2 and Capterra have a "Questions" tab on each product page. Filter by:

1. **Recently asked but unanswered.** Highest-leverage opportunity.
2. **Asked recently with weak vendor-only answers.** Your better answer can rank above the vendor's by getting "helpful" votes.
3. **Common evaluation questions** — *"Is X worth it?"*, *"How does X compare to Y?"*, *"Is the setup hard?"* These keep getting asked; your answer compounds.

For SuiteDash, focus categories:
- Onboarding & learning curve questions
- Integration questions (Stripe, Zapier, Mailchimp, etc.)
- Comparison questions (SuiteDash vs HoneyBook / Dubsado / etc.)
- Use case questions (consultants, agencies, solo)

Avoid:
- Pricing questions (vendor controls those answers and your input doesn't help)
- Technical bug questions (vendor has the answer; jumping in looks like overreach)

## Answer Structure

The best Q&A answers follow this structure:

```
[Direct answer to the question — one sentence]

[1–3 sentences of context that make the answer credible]

[1–2 sentences of practical advice the asker can act on]

[Optional: a concrete next step or what to read next]
```

**Example for SuiteDash:**

Q: *"How steep is the learning curve?"*

> The learning curve is real but well-defined: most operators need 8–15 hours of focused work to ship a basic deployment, and the friction comes from feature scope, not feature difficulty. Once you decide which 5–7 modules you're actually going to use (and ignore the rest), the rest of the platform becomes navigable in a couple of evenings.
>
> The trap is trying to learn everything before going live with a client. The way out is to pick one project type, configure that vertical slice, and ship it before you touch a second module.

That's substantive, useful, and lands the credibility marker. Profile click = funnel.

---

## What Not To Do

- ❌ Mention your product in the answer body. Profile-bio funneling only.
- ❌ Sound like a vendor. Buyers can tell. ("This product is amazing" → 0 clicks; "This product is solid for X but weak at Y" → 5x more clicks)
- ❌ Answer too many questions at once. 5 substantive answers in 14 days is better than 30 in one day (which gets flagged).
- ❌ Copy and paste between G2 and Capterra. Search engines penalize duplicate content; your authority compounds when each answer is original.
- ❌ Engage in arguments under existing answers. Answers are top-level only.

## Cadence

| Phase | Activity |
|---|---|
| Days -10 to -5 | 5 substantive G2 answers + 5 substantive Capterra answers, spread over 5 days |
| Days -4 to launch | 1 new answer every other day (low rate; you're building, not blasting) |
| Launch week | Pause new answers; reply to any comments on existing ones |
| Post-launch (steady state) | 1 answer per platform per week, indefinitely |

The total time investment is small (10–15 minutes per quality answer). The compounding return is high.

## Long-Term Effect

The compounding here is real:
- A G2/Capterra answer that gets 5–10 "helpful" votes ranks at the top of the Q&A indefinitely.
- Google indexes G2/Capterra Q&A pages and surfaces them in search results.
- Buyers in evaluation mode will read your answer for years.

For PDFs 2–7 (FLOWs, Circles, Automations, etc.), each new product is another wave of this work but the prior answers don't decay. By PDF 5 you'll have 50+ answers across both platforms — a permanent SEO + credibility asset.
