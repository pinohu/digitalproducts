# Lead Magnet Templates

Lead magnets capture emails. The job is to be just valuable enough that someone gives up their email, but not so big that it cannibalizes paid products.

## What Works (Patterns)

| Pattern | Format | Conversion Range |
|---|---|---|
| **One-page checklist** | 1 PDF page | 30–50% (highest) |
| **Decision framework / flowchart** | 1 PDF page | 25–40% |
| **Cheat sheet of curated tools / templates** | 1 PDF page | 25–40% |
| **5-day email course** | Email sequence | 20–35% |
| **Mini-PDF (5–10 pages)** | PDF | 15–30% |
| **Notion template** | Linked Notion page | 20–35% |
| **Spreadsheet / calculator** | Google Sheet | 20–35% |

## What Doesn't

- ❌ Anything over 30 pages — feels like work
- ❌ "Free course" with 12 modules — no one finishes
- ❌ "Definitive guide to [topic]" — too generic
- ❌ Quizzes that promise personalized results but deliver generic ones
- ❌ Webinars that require live attendance

## Lead Magnet Patterns (Steal Directly)

### Pattern 1: The "Skip This / Do This" Cheat Sheet

**Format:** 1-page PDF. Two columns. Left column: "What everyone says to do." Right column: "What actually works."

**Example title:** *"The SuiteDash Cheat Sheet: 12 Features to Skip + 7 to Master"*

**Why it works:** Counterintuitive, immediately actionable, signals expertise.

---

### Pattern 2: The Decision Flowchart

**Format:** 1 page. A flowchart that helps someone make a specific decision in their domain.

**Example title:** *"The Pricing Decision Flowchart: How to Price Any Digital Product in 90 Seconds"*

**Why it works:** Saves real time, gets bookmarked, gets shared.

---

### Pattern 3: The "Top 50 [X]" Curated List

**Format:** 2–3 page PDF. A curated list with a one-line note per item.

**Example title:** *"The Operator's Tool Stack: 50 Tools That Replace 4 Subscriptions"*

**Why it works:** High utility, reference-worthy, hard to replicate.

---

### Pattern 4: The 5-Day Email Course

**Format:** 5 emails over 5 days, each delivering one specific lesson.

**Example title:** *"5 Days to Your First Digital Product"*

**Why it works:** Builds the email habit (people see your name in their inbox 5 times) before you ever pitch them.

---

### Pattern 5: The Plug-and-Play Template

**Format:** A Notion template, Google Sheet, or Airtable base with linked instructions.

**Example title:** *"The Dynasty Operator's Notion Template: Project + Client + Revenue Tracking"*

**Why it works:** Higher perceived value (it's a *system*, not just info), and once they're using it, they're inside your ecosystem.

---

## The Lead Magnet → Product Bridge

The lead magnet should naturally point at a paid product without feeling like a sales pitch.

**The pattern:**
1. Lead magnet solves a *narrow* problem (the one-page version)
2. Final page of the lead magnet says: "If this was useful, [Product Name] goes deep on the full system."
3. Welcome email sequence (after signup) further teases the product without hard-selling

The lead magnet's job: **demonstrate competence** + **capture the email**. The product's job: **monetize the trust**.

---

## What to Charge for (Premium Lead Magnets)

For higher-quality lead magnets that take significant effort to produce, consider charging $9–$19 instead of giving them away. Counterintuitive, but:

- A $9 buyer is qualified (they have a credit card, they're willing to pay)
- A $9 buyer converts to a $49 product at 20–30%, vs. a free subscriber at 2–5%
- A $9 paid lead magnet creates a buyer-customer relationship from day one

Welsh's first product was $50, which he calls his "trust tripwire." A $9 paid lead magnet operates on the same principle.

## Setup Checklist

- [ ] Lead magnet PDF / template designed and exported
- [ ] Landing page live (single-purpose: collect email)
- [ ] Confirmation email delivers the lead magnet
- [ ] Welcome sequence (4–6 emails) introduces who you are and what you sell
- [ ] Conversion rate tracking in place (signups / page visits)
- [ ] Quarterly review: refresh the lead magnet if conversion drops below 15%
