# YouTube Comments Playbook

> *Companion to LinkedIn Playbook (`linkedin-playbook.md`). YouTube comment seeding on tutorial/review channels is the highest-density distribution surface for products where buyers are mid-learning-curve on a tool with an active video tutorial ecosystem (e.g., SuiteDash, ClickUp, Notion, HubSpot, Webflow).*

---

## When This Channel Wins

| Use this channel when... | Don't use this channel when... |
|---|---|
| Your buyer audience is actively learning a specific software tool | Your buyer is a generalist (founder, manager, marketer) without a specific tool focus |
| The tool has a YouTube tutorial channel with engaged comments (mid-3-figures+ comments per video) | The tool's official YouTube has dead comment sections |
| Your product compresses the learning curve | Your product is "more depth, more theory" — wrong fit for someone in skim-the-tutorials mode |
| Buyers concentrate in YouTube before they buy your product (PDF, course, template) | Buyers concentrate elsewhere (LinkedIn, Twitter, Slack communities) |

---

## The Mechanic

YouTube doesn't penalize promotional behavior the way other platforms do — but its algorithm DOES reward genuine engagement and DOES penalize linking-in-comments. The play is value-first commenting with profile-bio funneling.

When a viewer reads a substantive comment that helps them, a meaningful percentage will click your profile to see who you are. Your channel description / banner is the funnel surface. Direct link to your authority site (better) or to a pre-sell page (acceptable).

**Conversion math (rough estimates from analogous channels):**
- 1 substantive comment → 5–20 profile clicks (depends on video age + comment ranking)
- 100 profile clicks → 5–15 link-outs to your site
- 100 site visits → 2–5% conversion to email capture or pre-sell page
- 100 email captures or 100 pre-sell visitors → ~3% purchase rate at $49

**Working backward:** 20 substantive comments seeded over 14 days, on videos with 1k–10k views each, conservatively yields 3–8 sales by launch day before any other channel runs. That's not the launch — but it pays for the channel and de-risks Day 0.

---

## Selecting Videos

**Don't comment on every video on the channel.** Pick the 20 highest-leverage ones.

Criteria, ranked by importance:

1. **Active recent comments.** A video posted 2 years ago with comments still landing this week is gold — the algorithm is still surfacing it. Prefer these over fresh-but-quiet videos.
2. **Views in the 1k–50k range.** Below 1k = your comment won't get seen. Above 50k = comment competition is fierce; harder to land near the top.
3. **Specific topic in the title.** Comments on topical videos ("How to set up X automation") outperform comments on overview videos ("SuiteDash overview") by 3–5x. Specific topics attract specific audiences.
4. **Comment section has at least one confused question.** That's your scaffold opportunity — answer it.

## Profile Setup

The comment is the hook. Your profile is the conversion surface. Both must be configured before you start commenting.

**Channel banner:**
- Clean, professional, no clutter
- Tagline: short positioning statement (e.g., *"Operator playbooks for service businesses"* or *"Ship faster, configure less"*)
- Don't put a phone number, email address, or "Subscribe!" CTA in the banner — looks transactional

**Channel description:**
- One-line position statement
- One-line about-you with a credibility marker (years of experience, specific outcomes, trust signal)
- One link, max — to your authority site (preferred) or your pre-sell page
- No emoji-spam, no "🔥🔥🔥 NEW PRODUCT 🔥🔥🔥" framing

**About section (when applicable):**
- 2–3 paragraph bio
- The problems you solve, not the products you sell
- The link from the description, repeated

**Custom URL:**
- If your YouTube account qualifies, set the custom URL to a clean handle (`@YourName`)
- Saves the comment-reader one click vs. the random `UCxxxx` channel URL

## Cadence

- **Pre-launch seeding:** 1–2 comments per day for 14 days = 20 comments. Build profile authority before launch hits.
- **Launch week:** 2–3 new comments per day; reply to all comments on your prior posts that get new engagement.
- **Steady state (post-launch):** 5 comments per week, indefinitely. The compounding is real — old comments keep producing.

**Weekly review:**
- Which scaffold drove the most profile visits? (Visible in YouTube Studio)
- Which video produced the most pre-sell page traffic? (Visible in your site's analytics with referrer = youtube.com)
- Which comments got replies? Reply to those first next week.

---

## Anti-Patterns (Things That Get You Banned or Down-ranked)

- ❌ Linking in comments (profile bio only)
- ❌ Same scaffold repeated verbatim across multiple videos
- ❌ Commenting on every video on a channel within 24 hours (looks like a bot)
- ❌ Replies that are pure agreement ("Yes!" "So true!")
- ❌ Replies that contradict the video creator unprompted (you're a guest in their classroom)
- ❌ Replying to your own comment to bump it (algorithm catches this)
- ❌ Asking for likes / subs / engagement (the channel owner does that, not you)
