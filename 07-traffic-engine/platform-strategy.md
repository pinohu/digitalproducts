# Platform Strategy

The Rule of One: pick one platform where the buyer hangs out, master it, then expand. Don't spread thin across 5 platforms before any one is producing leads.

## Primary: LinkedIn

**Why:** B2B operators live here. The avatar (technical operators monetizing infrastructure) is on LinkedIn far more than they're on TikTok or Instagram. LinkedIn's organic reach for original content is also unusually strong vs. other platforms in 2026.

**Format mix:**
- 60% tactical posts (frameworks, lists, breakdowns)
- 20% case studies / personal stories
- 10% contrarian POV
- 10% behind-the-scenes / current work

**Cadence:** 3–5 posts per week, M/T/W/Th, morning ET (the LinkedIn algorithm rewards weekday morning posts heavily).

**What works on LinkedIn (2026):**
- Native posts > linked-out content (LinkedIn suppresses external links)
- 1,200–1,800 character text posts with line breaks every 1–2 sentences
- Lists and frameworks in carousel format (PDF carousels)
- Video clips (60–90 seconds, captioned)

**What doesn't:**
- Long quote-paragraphs
- Generic motivational content
- Engagement-bait ("agree?")
- Direct sales pitches (LinkedIn punishes obvious selling)

---

## Secondary: X / Twitter

**Why:** Faster content iteration, builders live there, easier to test ideas before committing them to LinkedIn.

**Format mix:**
- Daily one-liner observations
- 1–2 weekly thread breakdowns (5–10 tweets)
- Reply-guy strategy: thoughtful replies to bigger accounts in the niche

**Cadence:** Daily, 1–3 posts per day.

**What works on X:**
- Specific numbers ("I made $4,200 this week" > "I made some money")
- Counterintuitive openers
- Building in public — sharing process, not just outcomes
- Threads with 1 idea per tweet

---

## Long-Form Anchor: ikeohu.com

**Why:** Owned property. Discoverable via search. Establishes authority. Six-lane authority strategy is already in place; product launches reference essays.

**Cadence:** 1–2 essays per month, 1,500–3,500 words each.

**Content fit:**
- Frameworks too long for a LinkedIn post
- Case studies with screenshots / data
- The "definitive" version of a take that's been tested in shorter form on LinkedIn

**Linking strategy:** Every essay has a soft mention of relevant Dynasty Empire products near the end, plus a newsletter signup CTA.

---

## Tertiary (Use Selectively)

**Reddit:** Drop into r/SaaS, r/Solopreneur, r/SmallBusiness when you have something specific to share. Don't promote — share value, link only when asked.

**YouTube:** Long-form video is high-effort. Only worth it if you'll commit to weekly cadence. For now: don't.

**TikTok / Instagram Reels:** Wrong audience for this avatar. Skip.

---

## What Not To Do

- ❌ Cross-post identical content to all platforms — each platform has its own voice
- ❌ Try to grow on every platform simultaneously — pick one primary
- ❌ Buy followers / engagement — destroys algorithmic trust
- ❌ Outsource your voice — content from a ghostwriter reads as content from a ghostwriter
- ❌ Apply this system-default to every product without auditing — buyer density varies by product (see "Per-Product Override" below)

## Per-Product Override (Added 2026-05-08)

This file describes the **system default** for Dynasty's content distribution. The default is LinkedIn-primary because most Dynasty audiences (UX research portfolio, MSEAM curriculum, ikeohu.com authority site, operator strategy content) actually concentrate on LinkedIn.

But not every product's buyer audience is on LinkedIn. Before locking the launch playbook for a new product, run a **buyer-density audit**:

1. Count actual followers/members where the audience theoretically gathers (LinkedIn, Twitter, niche FB groups, in-app communities, review platforms)
2. Compare densities. If LinkedIn density is 10x the next-best channel, it's primary. If it's 1x or below, it's not.
3. Document findings in `/01-market-research/by-product/<slug>/distribution-findings.md`
4. If the audit overrides the LinkedIn-primary default, write a watering-holes addendum at `/06-launch-playbooks/by-product/<slug>-watering-holes.md`

**First product to use the override:** *The Good Parts of SuiteDash* — SuiteDash company LinkedIn page has 381 followers; AppSumo LTD audience and YouTube tutorial-channel commenters are an order of magnitude denser. Primary distribution mix shifted to YouTube comment seeding · G2/Capterra Q&A · AppSumo LTD FB groups · Upwork productized service · comparison-content affiliates · Reddit. LinkedIn stays as secondary credibility channel. See `/01-market-research/by-product/01-suitedash-good-parts/distribution-findings.md`.

Channel playbooks for the override channels: `/07-traffic-engine/youtube-comments-playbook.md`, `/07-traffic-engine/g2-capterra-qa-playbook.md`.

## The Quarterly Review

Every 90 days, look at:
- Which platform drove the most newsletter signups?
- Which platform drove the most direct product sales?
- Which platform takes the most time per signup / sale?

Cut what's underperforming. Double down on what's working. Don't keep posting on a platform out of habit.
