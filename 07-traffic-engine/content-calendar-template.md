# Content Calendar Template

Weekly content rotation that supports product sales without being salesy. Job: deliver 4 pieces of original content per week that build the audience and prime the next launch.

---

## The Default Week

| Day | Channel | Content Type | Purpose |
|---|---|---|---|
| Monday | LinkedIn | Tactical breakdown | Establish authority, drive bookmarks |
| Tuesday | X (thread) | List or framework | Reach new audience |
| Wednesday | LinkedIn | Case study / story | Build trust, social proof |
| Thursday | LinkedIn | Contrarian POV | Differentiation, pattern interrupt |
| Friday | Newsletter | Long-form synthesis | Deepen relationship with list |
| Saturday/Sunday | Off | Off | Rest |

## Monthly Layer

In addition to the weekly cadence, once per month:
- One **ikeohu.com essay** (long-form, 1,500–3,500 words)
- One **product mention** post that's primarily value but ends with a soft CTA
- One **community drop** (Reddit / Indie Hackers / niche Slack) where it's genuinely additive

## Quarterly Layer

Once per quarter:
- Refresh the lead magnet
- A multi-day "mini-launch" of an existing product with a new bonus or repositioning
- A "year-in-public" type retrospective post (transparency drives trust)

---

## Content Theme Buckets

Rotate themes so the audience gets variety, not the same topic from 5 angles:

1. **The Stack** — specific tools, configurations, automations
2. **The Mistake** — what went wrong + what was learned (high engagement)
3. **The Data** — numbers, charts, transparent metrics
4. **The Take** — opinion, contrarian POV
5. **The System** — frameworks, processes, repeatable methods
6. **The Story** — personal narrative tied to a business lesson

Aim for at least 4 of these 6 buckets each week.

---

## Idea Capture

Maintain a running file at `shared-assets/swipe-files/content-ideas.md` (placeholder exists) with every idea that surfaces. Most ideas come from:

- Buyer questions in email replies
- Friction points hit while building products
- Other creators' posts that you disagree with
- Searches you ran that returned bad results

When it's time to write, pull from the running list. Don't start from a blank page on a Monday morning.

---

## Production Workflow

For LinkedIn / X:
1. Sunday: outline 5 posts for the week (15 min)
2. Each morning before the post day: write the post (15 min)
3. Schedule via Taplio / Hypefury / native platform tools (5 min)
4. Throughout the day: respond to comments, DMs (15–30 min)

For newsletter:
1. Thursday: outline + draft (60 min)
2. Friday morning: edit + send (30 min)

Total content load: ~5 hours/week. Anything more is unsustainable; anything less is too thin to compound.

---

## Content That Drives Sales (Without Being Salesy)

The highest-converting content patterns:

- **"How I built X"** posts that mention the product as a byproduct
- **"What I learned shipping X"** retrospectives
- **"Here's the framework / here's the template"** giveaways that the product expands on
- **Testimonial-driven posts** ("Here's what someone said about [product]")

Avoid:
- Direct "BUY MY THING" posts
- Engagement bait that doesn't pay off in the body
- Posts that reference the product in every sentence
