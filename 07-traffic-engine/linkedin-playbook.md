# LinkedIn Playbook

LinkedIn is the primary platform. This is the tactical layer below `platform-strategy.md`.

## What LinkedIn Rewards (2026)

- Native posts (no external links)
- 1,200–1,800 character text posts
- Posts that drive *meaningful* comments (not "great post!" replies)
- Carousel PDFs (10-slide max, native upload)
- Short video (60–90 seconds, captioned)
- Engagement on others' posts in the first 60 minutes after they post

## What LinkedIn Punishes

- External links in the main post (cuts reach 40–60%)
- Engagement bait ("agree?" "thoughts?")
- Reposts of your own content within 7 days
- Excessive hashtags (>5 per post)
- @-tagging people who don't engage with your content

## Post Structures That Convert

### Structure 1: The List Post

```
[Hook line — counterintuitive or specific number]

[Setup — 1–2 lines of context]

Here's the list:

1. [Specific item with 1-line explanation]
2. [Specific item with 1-line explanation]
3. [Specific item with 1-line explanation]
...

[Closing line — one specific takeaway or question]
```

**Example hook:** *"I deleted 12 of the 19 SuiteDash modules I was using. Revenue went up 30%. Here's what I kept:"*

---

### Structure 2: The Story Post

```
[Specific moment / scene]

[3–5 sentences of narrative, building toward the lesson]

The lesson: [one sentence]

[Optional: how this applies to the reader]
```

**Example:** *"I lost a $4,200 client because I priced my work in dollars per hour..."*

---

### Structure 3: The Framework Post

```
[Bold claim or specific framework name]

Here's how it works:

→ Step 1: [verb + specific action]
→ Step 2: [verb + specific action]
→ Step 3: [verb + specific action]

[Closing line — when to use this / who it's for]
```

---

### Structure 4: The Contrarian Post

```
[Common belief everyone agrees with]

I disagree.

Here's what I actually do:

[3–5 specific behaviors that go against the grain]

[Closing line — why most advice is wrong about this]
```

## Post Frequency

- **3–5 posts per week** is the sweet spot
- Mon–Thu morning ET (algorithm rewards weekday morning posts)
- Avoid Friday afternoon and weekends (engagement drops 50%+)

## The First-Hour Strategy

The first 60 minutes after posting are critical for LinkedIn's algorithm. Within that window:

- Reply to every comment (drives more comments — algorithmic flywheel)
- Engage on 5–10 other posts in your niche (LinkedIn rewards reciprocity)
- Don't edit your post (kills reach)

## Comments > Likes

LinkedIn weights comments far more than likes. A post with 5 thoughtful comments often outperforms a post with 500 likes. Strategies to generate comments:

- End the post with an open-ended question (specific, not generic)
- Make a claim that invites pushback or qualification
- Share a specific story that resonates with shared experience
- Tag (sparingly) someone who's likely to add to the conversation

## Profile Optimization

- **Headline:** Outcome-focused, not job-title. "Helping operators monetize infrastructure they already own" > "Founder at Dynasty Empire"
- **Featured section:** Lead magnet + top 3 essays + main product page
- **About section:** Story-driven (your story) + clear positioning + clear next step (newsletter signup)
- **Activity:** Pin top 3 posts that demonstrate your range

## The Compounding Effect

LinkedIn growth is non-linear. The first 6 months feel slow. Then a single post breaks 100K impressions, and the trajectory shifts.

The compounding requires:
- Consistent cadence (3–5 posts/week, no gaps)
- Same niche, repeatedly (don't drift across topics)
- Engagement reciprocity (comment on others' work daily)
- Patience (12-month view, not 30-day view)
