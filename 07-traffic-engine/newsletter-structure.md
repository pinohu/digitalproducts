# Newsletter Structure

The weekly newsletter is the deepest trust-building asset in the stack. Long-form, no algorithm, owned audience. The newsletter is what differentiates a creator with a following from a creator with a business.

## Cadence

**Weekly. Friday morning. ~600–1,200 words.**

Don't skip weeks. The compounding effect requires consistency. Welsh hit 175K subscribers by sending every single Saturday for 5+ years.

## The Standard Format

```
[Subject Line — under 50 chars]

[Preview Line — under 80 chars, continues the subject]

[Header image or logo — optional]

Hook (1–2 sentences):
The specific insight or framing this email delivers.

Section 1 — The Setup (2–4 paragraphs):
The problem, the context, why this matters.

Section 2 — The Substance (3–5 paragraphs):
The framework, story, data, or breakdown that delivers the value.

Section 3 — The Takeaway (1–2 paragraphs):
One specific, actionable thing the reader can do this week.

Signature:
[Name]

P.S.:
Either a soft product mention OR a useful link OR a specific question that drives replies.
```

---

## Subject Line Patterns

- **Specific number:** *"3 SuiteDash automations I run for every client"*
- **Counterintuitive:** *"Why I stopped using Notion"*
- **Curiosity hook:** *"What a $4,200 invoice taught me about pricing"*
- **Question:** *"Is your tool stack actually saving you time?"*
- **Direct outcome:** *"How to ship a digital product in 14 days"*

Subject lines that fail: vague ("This week's update"), salesy ("Don't miss this!"), generic ("Some thoughts on...").

---

## Voice and Tone

- **Direct.** No throat-clearing. The first sentence should land.
- **Specific.** Name names, name tools, name dollar amounts. Generic content reads as filler.
- **First-person.** "I" not "we." It's a one-person business pretending to be a one-person business.
- **Conversational, not corporate.** Write like you'd write to a friend who's also building.

---

## Sales Mentions

The default ratio: **3 nurture-only newsletters for every 1 with a sales mention.**

When you mention a product:
- One sentence in the body, not a full pitch
- A P.S. with a soft link
- "If you want the deep version of this, [Product Name] expands on it"

Hard pitches are reserved for launch sequences. The newsletter is for trust-building.

---

## Length

| Length | When to Use |
|---|---|
| 400–600 words | Quick frameworks, one-shot insights |
| 600–1,200 words | Default — most newsletters live here |
| 1,200–2,000 words | Deep dives, case studies |
| 2,000+ | Save for ikeohu.com essays, not newsletters |

Anything over 1,500 words risks losing the casual reader. Better to split into two newsletters than to overload one.

---

## The Welcome Sequence (For New Subscribers)

When someone joins the list (via lead magnet or direct signup), they enter a 5-email welcome sequence before joining the regular Friday cadence:

1. **Day 0 — Welcome + lead magnet delivery + brief intro**
2. **Day 2 — Origin story / why this newsletter exists**
3. **Day 4 — One of your best-performing past essays / frameworks**
4. **Day 6 — Soft mention of your most relevant product, no hard pitch**
5. **Day 8 — Bridge into the regular weekly cadence**

After Day 8, they get the regular Friday newsletter.

## Performance Targets

| Metric | Floor | Target | Best |
|---|---|---|---|
| Open rate | 25% | 40% | 60%+ |
| Click rate | 2% | 7% | 15%+ |
| Reply rate | 0.5% | 2% | 5%+ |
| Unsubscribe (per send) | <0.5% | <0.3% | <0.1% |
| Sponsor inquiry rate (when list >5K) | — | 1–2/quarter | — |

---

## Platforms

For first 1–2K subscribers: **Gumroad's native subscriber feature** works fine. Free, integrated.

When list crosses ~2K subscribers OR when you want segmentation, custom domains, paid tiers, or sponsorship integrations: graduate to **Beehiiv** (recommended), **ConvertKit**, or **Substack**.

Don't graduate too early. Optimization is a distraction when you have <1K subscribers.

---

## What This Newsletter Is *Not*

- Not a roundup of links from around the web (no original thought = list churn)
- Not a recap of your social media posts (subscribers already saw those)
- Not a "personal blog" with whatever crossed your mind (no consistent reader value)

It's a weekly value delivery vehicle for a specific audience, anchored to a specific topic, that ladders into a specific product line.
