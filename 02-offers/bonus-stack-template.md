# Bonus Stack Template

Bonuses are not "extras." They are the offer's primary tool for objection handling and value amplification. A well-constructed bonus stack can take a $49 PDF to a $97-perceived offer with no additional core content.

## The Rules

1. **Each bonus addresses a specific objection.** "But what if I don't know X?" → Bonus that solves X.
2. **Each bonus has a standalone dollar value.** Stated explicitly in the offer ("$47 value").
3. **Bonuses must be deliverable inside the same Gumroad product.** No separate logins, no "we'll email it later." Day-one delivery only.
4. **The bonus stack should add to 5–10x the price.** $49 product → $250–$500 in stacked bonus value.
5. **Bonuses must be *useful*, not filler.** A 200-page "ultimate guide to nothing" PDF is not a bonus; it's noise.

## Naming Your Bonuses

Hormozi's MAGIC formula for naming. Boring bonus names destroy stacked value. Compare:

❌ **"Email Templates"** → vs. → ✅ **"The 12 Cold-Reply Scripts That Booked 47 Demos in Q1"**

❌ **"Setup Guide"** → vs. → ✅ **"The 90-Minute SuiteDash Lock-In Protocol"**

The name should:
- Suggest a specific, quantified outcome
- Reference time, count, or other anchors
- Sound like a product, not a freebie

## Delivery Format

In Gumroad, all bonuses can be delivered in two ways:
1. **Bundled into the main file** (best for PDFs that include linked supporting docs)
2. **Multiple files attached to the same product** (cleanest, lets buyers download what they want)

Default to option 2 unless there's a reason to consolidate.
